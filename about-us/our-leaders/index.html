Teo Lay Lim
Deputy Chairman and CEO

Teo Lay Lim

Deputy Chairman and CEO

Ms Teo Lay Lim is the Chief Executive Officer of SPH Media Group, and also serves on the board of SPH Media Holdings as Deputy Chair. Prior to joining SPH Media, Ms Teo held various leadership positions at Accenture for over thirty years until her retirement on 31 August 2021. She is currently on the boards of UOB, Workforce Singapore, and the Singapore Accountancy Commission. Ms Teo also chairs the Polytechnic & ITE External Review Panel for the Ministry of Education, and is on the Advisory Board for SMU’s College of Integrative Studies.

Wong Wei Kong
Editor-in-Chief, English/Malay/ Tamil Media Group

Wong Wei Kong

Editor-in-Chief, English/Malay/ Tamil Media Group

Wei Kong was appointed Editor-in-Chief of the English, Malay and Tamil Media group of SPH Media in October 2022, overseeing The Straits Times, The Business Times, and the vernaculars, Berita Harian and Tamil Murasu. He was Editor of The Business Times from December 2016 to October 2022, managing the editorial as well as non-editorial operations of the paper.

Wei Kong served as The Business Times’ Executive Editor from 2014 to 2016, and prior to that, was its News Editor for three years. He served as Associate News Editor from 2006 to 2011. He undertook a major revamp of The Business Times’ print, digital and mobile editions in 2014, as well as the relaunch of its Weekend edition in 2016. Following that, The Business Times launched two new content verticals, Asean Business, and Garage, for startups. In 2022, the paper reorganised itself into a digital-first newsroom that also produces a high-quality print product. He joined The Business Times in 1998.

Wei Kong is a member of the Corporate Governance Advisory Committee set up by the Monetary Authority of Singapore to promote good corporate governance, and as Editor of The Business Times, he led BT’s involvement in several major awards that seek to encourage business excellence. This includes the Singapore Business Awards, the Singapore Corporate Awards, the Emerging Enterprise Awards, and the Enterprise 50 Awards. He is a board member of The Rice Company Ltd, an arts-focused social enterprise, and is also Chairman of the Business Times’ Budding Artists Fund, which seeks to uplift underprivileged children and youths through the arts.

Wei Kong graduated from the National University of Singapore with a Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in History.

Lee Huay Leng
Editor-in-Chief, Chinese Media Group

Lee Huay Leng

Editor-in-Chief, Chinese Media Group

Huay Leng is the Editor-in-Chief for the Chinese Media Group in SPH Media Group. She is responsible for Lianhe Zaobao and its global digital platform zaobao.com, the evening daily Shin Min Daily News, a stable of Student Publications, a digital channel for young audience called HeyKaki and an English language e-magazine called ThinkChina.sg. 

Huay Leng joined SPH in 1994 and held numerous roles as a journalist, Hong Kong Correspondent, Beijing Bureau Chief, China Editor, News Editor and Deputy Editor of Lianhe Zaobao. She helmed the evening daily Lianhe Wanbao from 2014-2016. In Dec 2016, she was appointed Head of the Chinese Media Group and was re-designated to her current role with the formation of SPH Media Trust in 2021. 

Huay Leng currently serves as a member of the Public Transport Council, Founders’ Memorial Committee and The Esplanade Co Ltd. She is also a board director of the Lee Kuan Yew Fund for Bilingualism and Business China. She received the Hsing Yun Journalism Award (Outstanding Achievement) in 2019.

Huay Leng graduated with an honours degree in Chinese Studies from the National University of Singapore and holds a Master of Arts degree in Southeast Asian Studies from the School of Oriental and African Studies, University of London. She was an asian fellow at Ash Center for Democratic Governance and Innovation, Harvard Kennedy School (2010-2011), and a Singapore Lien fellow of Lien Ying Chow Legacy Fellowship (2014).

Ignatius Low
Editor-in-Chief, Lifestyle and Entertainment Media Group

Ignatius Low

Editor-in-Chief, Lifestyle and Entertainment Media Group

Ignatius heads the newly-formed Lifestyle and Media Entertainment Group within SPH Media. This consists of SPH Media’s broadcast radio, lifestyle media and out-of-home businesses, all of which extend SPH Media’s reach to non-news consuming audiences, especially the young.

Run as standalone businesses in the past, the Group is home to well-established titles such as Her World, HardwareZone and Home & Decor and Harper’s Bazaar, FM radio stations Kiss92 and UFM1003 and Singapore’s only business news and information station, MoneyFM89.3. Its out-of-home network, SPHMBO, includes prominent outdoor digital screens in the financial district and the Orchard Road shopping belt, as well as indoor screen networks within malls like Vivocity and national retail networks such as Fairprice.

A former PSC scholar that read Politics, Philosophy & Economics at Oxford University, Ignatius first joined SPH as a finance correspondent at The Straits Times in 1999. Since then, he has taken on a mix of both editorial and commercial challenges at SPH. He has led both local and business news coverage at ST and was also the paper’s Managing Editor, handling events and branding, schools outreach, commercial sponsorships and strategic partnerships. He also wrote a fortnightly column in The Sunday Times for 12 years and has authored or co-authored four books, including two on Singapore’s financial sector and the Hard Truths interviews with the late Minister Mentor Lee Kuan Yew.

Prior to his current appointment, Ignatius headed SPH Media’s advertising business as Chief Commercial Officer for more than four years where he restructured SPH’s ad sales and content marketing teams into fully integrated omni-channel ad solutions units. He also served on a number of public sector committees in areas of built heritage (URA’s Conservation Advisory Panel and Architectural Heritage Awards Assessment Committee) and national service (Mindef’s ACCORD).

Andy is the Chief Financial Officer of SPH Media Group. He is responsible for the strategic planning and management of all financial endeavours of Singapore’s largest omnichannel media network.

David Pang
Chief Transformation Officer

David Pang

Chief Transformation Officer

David is the Chief Transformation Officer of SPH Media Group. He is responsible for long-range strategic planning, transformation programmes, business intelligence, strategic partnerships and organisational change management for Singapore’s largest omnichannel media network as it embarks on the next chapter of renewal and innovation.

Prior to SPH Media, he held concurrent global leadership roles at Shiseido Travel Retail, Shiseido Group as Vice President, Business Development & Strategy, Vice President, Human Resources, and Chief of Staff.

With over two decades of professional experience, David was also responsible for commercial and consulting engagements as well as advising Fortune 500 companies in the beauty, financial services, pharma, and energy sectors on business transformation, operating model design, organisational strategy, and change management.

As a thought-leader, David has been featured by BBC News on the topic of ‘Digital Agility’, Channel NewsAsia for commentaries on ‘Attracting millennial talent’ and ‘Learning Agility’ as well as the South China Morning Post on ‘What Industry 4.0 means to Singapore and why its workers must upskill and lose their sense of entitlement.’

David graduated from Nanyang Business School (Nanyang Technological University) with a degree in Applied Economics and attended the Senior Leadership Program at IESE Business School.

Joanna Ho

Chief Commercial & Marketing Officer

Kaythaya Maw

Kaythaya Maw

Chief Technology Officer

Jaime Ho
Editor, The Straits Times

Jaime Ho

Editor, The Straits Times

Jaime is the Editor of The Straits Times, where he is responsible for all aspects of the newsroom’s operations and growth.

Before joining SPH Media Group in October 2022, Jaime’s career had spanned more than 25 years across journalism and the Singapore civil service. He served in the Ministry of Foreign Affairs between 1997 to 2013, overseeing issues such as bilateral relations with Europe and climate change, while also serving in Washington, D.C. and Geneva.

Jaime joined Mediacorp in 2013 as Associate Editor before becoming Deputy Chief Editor. He was appointed Chief Editor of CNA Digital in January 2016, a role he held until April 2022. Under his leadership, the team successfully navigated a period of significant audience growth, and the platform won several accolades. Jaime also wrote extensively at Mediacorp and fronted several broadcast events for CNA.

From June to October 2022, Jaime was also a Partner at FGS Global, a global strategic advisory, where he led its Singapore office and its operations locally and regionally.

Jaime graduated from the National University of Singapore with a Bachelor of Arts (Hons) in 1997. He also holds a Master in Public Management from the Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy in 2006.

Chen Huifen

Chen Huifen

Editor, The Business Times

Huifen is the Editor of The Business Times (BT) of SPH Media Group. She is responsible for upholding the journalistic quality and integrity of the products under the BT brand. Mohamed Sa’at Abdul Rahman

Mohamed Sa’at Abdul Rahman

Editor, Berita Harian

Sa’at is the Editor, Berita Harian (BH) of SPH Media Group. He manages the only Malay-language newspaper in Singapore and is responsible for the digital and print content that serves Malay/Muslim readers.
